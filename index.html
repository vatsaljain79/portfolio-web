Mastering Competitive Programming - Strategy

Competitive programming is not about sudden brilliance; it is about consistent improvement over time. The right strategy focuses on practice, patience, and learning from every contest and problem you attempt.

Practice and Practice

The most important rule of competitive programming is simple - practice regularly. Problem-solving skills develop only through continuous exposure to different types of questions. The more problems you solve, the stronger your intuition and pattern recognition become.

Consistency is the Key

Consistency matters more than solving many problems occasionally. Even small daily practice sessions help maintain momentum and gradually improve speed and accuracy. Long-term consistency leads to significant improvement.

Participate in Contests Frequently

Try to give as many contests as possible across different platforms. Competing regularly helps optimize your time management, improves thinking under pressure, and exposes you to diverse problem styles.

Do Not Feel Low After Bad Contests

Not every contest will go well, and that is completely normal. Poor performances are part of the learning process. Instead of getting discouraged, analyze mistakes and stay consistent with practice.

Learn from Unsolved Problems

If you are unable to solve a question during practice, spend time understanding its tutorial or editorial. If a new concept appears, make sure you understand it properly and remember the approach so that you can solve similar problems independently in the future.

  • Practice regularly and stay consistent.
  • Participate in contests on multiple platforms.
  • Do not get discouraged by bad performances.
  • Learn actively from editorials and new concepts.
  • Focus on long-term improvement rather than short-term results.

Internship Interview Day at IIT Delhi - Aug 3, 2024 | Day 1

Before Day 1 of internship interviews, I was shortlisted for almost all company Online Assessments, and after the OAs, I had shortlists from more than 10 companies scheduled for Day 1. It felt exciting - but also extremely overwhelming.

The Day Begins - 6:00 AM

Day 1 started early at 6 AM on August 3rd with my first interview at Graviton for the ULL role. Unfortunately, I could not perform at my best in Round 1 itself and got eliminated. It was not the start I had hoped for, but the day had only just begun.

Next came two rounds of interviews with Millennium followed by three rounds with Goldman Sachs. Later, I also received an interview call from DE Shaw. Although my interviews with Goldman Sachs and Millennium went well, I was not selected.

By 12 PM, Slot 1 was over - multiple interviews completed, but no offer in hand. I felt disappointed and honestly a bit depressed. However, I knew the most important thing at that moment was to stay calm and prepare for Slot 2 starting at 3 PM.

Slot 2 - The Long Evening

The second half of the day began with an interview at Adobe Research, followed by Round 1 and Round 2 interviews with Atlassian. Later, I received a call from Samsung Korea, where after three rounds, I was placed on the offer waitlist.

Then came three rounds with Barclays, where again I was placed on the offer waitlist. Around 8:30 PM, after most companies had finalized selections, I started an interview with QRT. I cleared Round 1 but was rejected after Round 2.

After an entire day filled with interviews, I still had no offer. I returned to my hostel feeling completely exhausted and mentally drained.

The Night

A friend came to my room and asked whether I had eaten anything. I realized I had not eaten since morning. We went to the canteen together, had dinner, and I finally went to sleep reminding myself that I still had interviews scheduled for the next day - more than five shortlists waiting.

Despite everything, I believed that whatever happens, happens for a reason.

The Morning That Changed Everything

The next morning, I woke up at 5 AM and checked my phone. There was a message from the OCS team:

“You have been selected by Atlassian. Congratulations!”

That moment is impossible to describe in words - the contrast between a hopeless night and a clear, hopeful morning. All the stress, uncertainty, and emotions of the previous day suddenly made sense.

Final Thought

Life does not always reward you immediately. Sometimes you go through failures, rejections, and long exhausting days before things fall into place. Just believe in yourself, stay patient, and keep moving forward.

  • Rejections are temporary.
  • Consistency and calmness matter under pressure.
  • Every experience prepares you for the right opportunity.
  • Whatever happens eventually works out for the best.

Placement Interview Day - Dec 1, 2025 | Day 1

Before Day 1 of placements, I had already completed all three interview rounds with Databricks. The interviews went well, but unfortunately I was not selected - possibly because of my CGPA (8.8). Around the same time, I had also completed Round 1 and Round 2 with Squarepoint, both of which went extremely well. With strong hopes, I entered Day 1 on December 1st.

6:00 AM - The Day Begins

The morning started early with Round 3 at Squarepoint for a Software Developer position. Unfortunately, the interview did not go as expected. Without much time to think, I immediately moved to my next interview at DE Shaw.

I had two rounds there, but later I was informed that they were moving forward with other candidates. Soon after, I headed to Goldman Sachs, where I faced three back-to-back interviews. After completing all rounds, I was placed on the waitlist.

By 11:30 AM, multiple interviews were done - yet I still had no offer in hand. The pressure and uncertainty were real, but the day was not over yet.

The Turning Point

I then moved to JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPMC). After another series of three consecutive interview rounds, the moment finally arrived - I received an offer.

That feeling is impossible to fully describe. The joy, relief, and satisfaction of finally being placed after continuous interviews and uncertainty made every effort worth it. All the preparation, failures, and persistence finally paid off.

Final Reflection

Placement journeys test not only your technical skills but also your patience, mental strength, and belief in yourself. Rejections are temporary, but persistence eventually leads you to the right opportunity.

  • Every rejection teaches something valuable.
  • Stay calm even when things don’t go your way.
  • Trust your preparation and keep moving forward.
  • Your moment will come - sometimes when you least expect it.
